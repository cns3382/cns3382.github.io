Ethical Issues

Hacking, including hackathons and other education-related events, is an extremely unethical way to obtain information or money. Crypto-currencies are not designed to aid hackers; they were intended to allow people to pay for things online without relying on institutions to supervise transactions, yet they have been hacked.The problem with crypto-currencies is that anyone with a computer, an internet connection, and hacking skills might theoretically acquire access to money that does not belong to them. It's a security concern, and there will continue to be problems until block chains and bitcoin exchange companies can tighten their grip on the bitcoins in their possession. Volatility is not always an ethical concern, but it is a significant concern with currencies like bitcoin. If the crypto-currency can maintain a stable value, it will be able to attract more users. Its appeal would readily increase if it could stabilise at a higher level of worth.
